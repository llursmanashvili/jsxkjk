The BestSellers

Versace Eros

Versace's Eros perfume is a radiant masculinity. Intense, lively and sparkling, derived from a combination of mint leaves, Italian lemon zest and green apple. Addictive sensuality created by oriental, intriguing and exotic notes such as tonka bean, amber, geranium flower and vanilla.

Armani Code

The elegant and sophisticated perfume Armani Code is intended for those who are always open to new experiences and confidently reach the heights of success. The trio of famous perfumers managed to find the code for the multifaceted character of modern men with a charisma that is simply impossible to resist.

Scandal

The perfume Jean Paul Gaultier Scandal breaks all clichés, offering something new and modern, yet powerful and elegant, and its advertising campaign was created as a provocative mix of sex and politics. The top notes of a wonderfully seductive composition are juicy orange, honey, patchouli and gardenia, combining a creamy, sensual deep earthy and balsamic gourmand effect with a warm spicy woody undertone.

Intenso

Men's perfume Dolce & Gabbana Pour Homme Intenso is pure instinct, the power of freely expressed emotion. This is the strength of a determined and confident person, wholeheartedly loyal to tradition but sweetened by modernity. This woody aromatic perfume opens with clear aquatic notes with green accords of basil and lavender. In the heart there is a masculine aroma of tobacco, sage and cypress notes. A combination of incense, sandalwood and a unique mopel accord in the base reveals rich floral, woody and honey tones.

Perfume History

The ancient Greeks can boast of making the first liquid perfumes. But it was the development of distillation by the Arabs that made perfume production viable. Perfume enjoyed great success in the seventeenth century, especially in France.
